## What Is Inspected

One inspection is limited to:

1. one named tokenized fund or share class;
2. one named collateral receiver, custodian, prime broker, protocol, CCP or
   secured party;
3. one governing collateral schedule, admission policy or equivalent control;
4. one fixed observation and decision boundary; and
5. one intended financial use.

The control determines whether the available evidence establishes that the
exact instrument can be identified, transferred, controlled, valued, corrected
and enforced within the named workflow at the named boundary.

The determination is `PASS`, `PARTIAL`, `FAIL` or `UNKNOWN`. `UNKNOWN` is a
valid result when mandatory evidence is unavailable. No missing fact is
inferred and no admission decision is manufactured.

## Included Output

- financial-object and authoritative-record receipt;
- token-role and transfer-effectiveness determination;
- custody, control, reconciliation and correction map;
- valuation, haircut and concentration evidence receipt;
- program-specific admission precheck;
- FINOS CDM eligibility-query input, or a machine-readable hold record;
- contradiction and Unknown register;
- rights-safe written determination and machine-readable JSON;
- one factual correction cycle; and
- final source, calculation and delivery hashes.

The inspection is a decision input. It is not a legal opinion, audit,
valuation opinion, investment recommendation or automatic collateral approval.

1. legal name of the commissioning organization;

4. exact receiving workflow and decision owner;
5. governing admission policy or collateral schedule;
6. required observation boundary and decision deadline;

8. list of restricted records the buyer controls or can authorize; and
9. invoicing contact and preferred procurement route.
