# FET TFII SEC EDGAR Rights Basis 001

**Reference family:** Tokenized Financial Instrument Integrity Reference Family 001  
**Status:** Controlled rights determination  
**Boundary date:** 15 July 2026  
**Operator:** Darwin Rampai, independent researcher

## 1. Question

May FET cite, transform, display, and distribute bounded factual records derived
from the five public SEC EDGAR filings used by TFII 001 without requesting a
separate permission from the SEC?

## 2. Official Policy Evidence

The controlling policy source for this determination is the SEC's Website
Dissemination statement in its [Privacy Information](https://www.sec.gov/about/privacy-information).
The SEC states that sec.gov information is public information and may be copied
or further distributed without separate SEC permission. The policy recommends
appropriate attribution and separately restricts the SEC seal, logos, and
registered marks in uses that imply affiliation or approval.

The normalized, hash-bound extraction receipt remains controlled.

## 3. Bounded Determination

Within the reviewed policy boundary, FET may:

- cite and link to the five official EDGAR filings;
- retain local source receipts for reproducibility;
- extract and normalize filing facts;
- publish bounded excerpts with attribution;
- publish FET-created classifications, comparisons, diagrams, and records; and
- distribute or license FET-created EDGAR-derived structure and methodology.

No separate SEC permission request is required for those listed uses under the
SEC's published dissemination policy.

## 4. Excluded Rights

This determination does not provide:

- issuer, sponsor, adviser, transfer-agent, custodian, or SEC endorsement;
- permission to use the SEC seal, EDGAR logo, issuer marks, or fund names as
  branding that implies affiliation or approval;
- rights to private operational records, workpapers, legal opinions, customer
  records, or confidential correspondence;
- rights to unrelated market data, identifiers, terminal content, or downstream
  datasets controlled by another provider;
- permission to expose personal data merely because it appears in a filing; or
- a jurisdiction-wide legal opinion for every possible product or use.

Any future non-EDGAR input requires its own source-specific rights state.

## 5. Product Boundary

The rights-safe TFII public and commercial unit is FET-created structure:

- canonical instrument-integrity records;
- evidence and Unknown classifications;
- authoritative-record and transfer-boundary comparisons;
- source locators and source hashes;
- FET methodology and correction history; and
- bounded excerpts needed to reproduce the classification.

The product is not a repackaged archive of complete source filings. Official
filings remain linked to their SEC source.

The former blanket statement that public access did not establish a reuse basis
was too broad for these five SEC filings. The corrected state is bounded:
EDGAR-derived TFII records have a public reuse basis; private, branded, and
third-party inputs remain separately controlled.

## 7. Release Effect

This determination closes the general SEC-source reuse blocker for the bounded
TFII package. It does not by itself authorize release. Bounded public field
extraction and one downstream-use chain are complete; named-subject factual
review, final rendering, and hash freeze remain separate controls.

This document records FET's source-policy determination. It is not legal advice
to another party.
